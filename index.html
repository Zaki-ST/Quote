Aqua-Lab Pavilion 12 Interactive Stations

  • Detailed Exhibit Breakdown A premium, tightly integrated aquatic physics lab teaching fluid dynamics and collaborative engineering in a condensed footprint.
    1. Archimedes Screw Lift
    An ancient mechanical device used to lift water from a lower basin to an upper reservoir by turning a helical surface inside a pipe.
    2. Laminar Jumping Jets
    Smooth, perfectly clear, glass-like tubes of water that leap through the air without splashing until they hit a surface or a child's hand.
    3. Vortex Generator
    A controllable whirlpool enclosed in acrylic where children alter the flow and insert plastic balls to see how vortexes pull objects down.
    4. Modular Dam Table
    A large stream table with slot-in barriers. Kids work together to redirect water flow, build reservoirs, and prevent flooding on a miniature landscape.
    5. Pneumatic Shooters
    Push-button air blasters that launch sudden jets of water at spinning targets, teaching pneumatic pressure and mechanical reactions.
    6. Hydro-Power Wheel
    A water wheel attached to an LED display. As children direct more water over the wheel, the lights brighten, demonstrating kinetic energy conversion.
    7. Buoyancy Tanks
    Children pump air into sunken "submarine" vessels to displace water and watch them rapidly rise to the surface, teaching density.
    8. Wave Generator
    A crank-operated paddle that generates mechanical waves in a long, clear trough, showing how energy moves through liquids.
    9. Fluid Musical Organ
    A series of vertical pipes that emit different musical notes depending on the level of water inside them when children strike the tops with paddles.
    10. Water Bell Fountains
    Water forced upward over a metal dome, creating a perfect, unbroken bell shape of water that children can safely touch without breaking the flow.
    11. Rain Condenser
    A chilled metal structure that collects ambient room humidity, causing droplets to form and "rain" down, teaching the natural water cycle.
    12. Toddler "Dew Drop"
    A safe, shallow, low-flow bubbling basin exclusively designed for infants and toddlers to safely splash and explore textures.
  • Size Constraints Footprint: 100 sqm | Recommended Total Zone: 180 sqm (includes ADA dry-off and circulation)
  • Cost & Sourcing Purchase: $350,000 – $600,000 USD.
    Top Fabricators: Boss Display, Hüttinger Interactive.
